About us and our History

The University offers about 100 curricular programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels. It comprises 1,200 faculty and staff members from all schools and more than 25,000 students. In compliance with the Expanded Tertiary Education and Accreditation Program (ETEEAP), the University is also permitted to certify and bestow the relevant academic degrees. The National Center for Research and Development for Coffee and Urban Agriculture has been designated by the Department of Agriculture and is accredited by TESDA as a Trade and Testing Venue. As part of its mission to become "the premier University in historic Cavite recognized for excellence in the development of globally competitive and morally upright individuals," CvSU also houses the Southern Tagalog Agriculture Research and Development Consortium (STARDEC), the Affiliated Renewable Energy Center for Region IV-A, and the Regional ICT Center for Region IV-A.
